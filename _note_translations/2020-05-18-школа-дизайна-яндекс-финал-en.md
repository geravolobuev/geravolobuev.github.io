## The main points

**What I learned as a result of the course:**

- That an idea “in your head” is fucking useless to anyone and means nothing; execution is what matters.

- The importance of iterations. As a rule, nothing worthwhile comes out on the first try. Conceived-researched-made-learned-(repeated)—shipped.

- A well-defined task and criteria for successful completion will save a lot of time spent designing unnecessary features.

- About the importance of deadlines and time control (time management, etc.)

- That the designer themself, their thoughts, desires, and pains are not representative as a model of the product’s future user.

- That moderately beautiful and convenient is better than merely beautiful, or beautiful and not very convenient (or not even very necessary).

- **About the importance of answering the question “why” at any stage, any step in developing a product and its features.**

The most useful lectures in terms of information were those by Anton Shein, Taras Shevchenko, Konstantin Gorsky, and Dmitry Bykov.
